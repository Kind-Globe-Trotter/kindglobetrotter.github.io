Summer in Toronto

By Kind Globe Trotter
February 1, 2021

Canada is known for its cold winters, with temperatures going way below 0°C. However, summers can be surprisingly hot 🔥

Join us to discover Toronto's summer, where the blue sky and the heat will make you want to take a dip in Lake Ontario 🌊

Harbourfront

One of the best things to do on a hot summer day in Toronto is to wander near the Lake Ontario, among the joggers, dog-walkers and tourists enjoying the sun.

Going westwards along the lake, you will enter Toronto Music Garden, near the marina. This peaceful little garden, filled with birds, butterflies and gorgeous plants, is ideal for an evening stroll.

City Centre

Toronto is a multicultural city, which perfectly combines new buildings with older architecture. Near the lake, the CN Tower and the skyscrappers are a constant reminder that the city centre is built to reach high altitudes.

However, among this extremely urban landscape, there is place for pieces of architecture that could belong in any European city's old town.

This perfect combination of modern and historic architecture makes Toronto a very enjoyable city to walk in, despite its traffic and high density of tall office buildings.

Ripley's Aquarium of Canada

After a long day's walk in the streets of Toronto, an indoor activity is always welcome. In the CN Tower's shadow lies the Ripley's Aquarium of Canada, whose most famous attraction is this underwater tunnel where sharks and rays can be observed, among others.

💡 Coeliac Corner

One of us is coeliac, so we cannot really give any advice about regular restaurants. However, we can recommend Almond Butterfly, on Harbord, where you will find 100% gluten-free pastries and bagels. We particularly enjoyed the blueberry pancake stack 😋
